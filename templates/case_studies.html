Coordinating a Three-Way PC Replacement Without Disrupting Business

SR Ticket: INC0031234 Client: Banana Company, Inc. Project Owner: George Smith Timeline: Jul 20 – Aug 11, 2026
The Note

Replacing a single computer is usually straightforward. Coordinating three different computers, multiple users, leasing deadlines, data migration, procurement, and business operations — all at the same time — is an entirely different challenge.

One project I frequently managed involved what I called a Three-Way PC Replacement. Unlike a standard PC refresh, every device depended on another device being available at exactly the right time.

The Scenario

User A (New Employee)
A new employee was scheduled to start at the beginning of the month. A brand-new leased laptop (PC1) had already arrived at the office. However, rather than assigning the new device directly to the new hire, management decided to reallocate assets more efficiently.

The new employee would instead receive PC3, a shared office computer that had been repurposed and reimaged specifically for them. Because the shared PC was still used daily by office staff, it could not be removed until the morning of the employee's first day. That left only a few hours to:

  • Back up all remaining shared data
  • Reimage the computer
  • Configure corporate applications
  • Restore required data
  • Deliver the computer before the afternoon

User B (Department Manager)
The department manager wanted to replace their existing computer (PC2) with the brand-new leased PC (PC1). Although the new computer had already been delivered, the migration had to wait until the manager had enough availability for data transfer and application testing.

Timing was important. The old leased PC needed to be retired before the lease billing period ended to avoid unnecessary monthly charges.

Shared Department PC
The department also maintained a common-use computer for employees who needed access to a specialized application. Recently, that application had migrated to a web-based SaaS platform. Since employees could now access the application from their own computers, the department decided to eliminate the shared PC to reduce monthly leasing costs and improve security.

Before retiring the machine:

  • Department data had to be backed up to the shared file server
  • Required files needed to be preserved
  • The computer had to remain available until the morning of the new employee's first day

The Challenge

Although only three computers were involved, the project required careful scheduling. Each task depended on another task finishing first:

  • Shared PC data had to be backed up before reimaging
  • The new employee's computer had to be ready before the afternoon of Day 1
  • The manager's migration had to occur before lease expiration
  • The retired computer had to complete the organization's decommissioning process
  • Procurement needed confirmation that lease assets had been returned on time
  • Management required progress updates throughout the project
A delay at any step could affect multiple users, create additional leasing costs, or postpone a new employee's onboarding.

What I Learned

Many people think desktop support is simply replacing computers. In reality, enterprise PC refresh projects involve coordinating multiple teams, including:

  • Office Administration
  • Procurement
  • IT Support
  • Department Managers
  • End Users
  • Asset Management

Success depends not only on technical skills but also on planning, communication, scheduling, and understanding the entire asset lifecycle. One missed deadline can lead to unnecessary leasing charges, delayed onboarding, or business disruption.

Good IT support is not just fixing computers — it is making sure the right computer reaches the right person at exactly the right time.
Milestones & Task Breakdown
Service Desk / Service Request Management
  • Receive the customer's PC replacement request. Create a Service Request (SR) ticket.
  • Coordinate the replacement schedule with the customer, procurement team, and engineering team.
  • Assign the SR ticket to the Engineering team.
  • Monitor task progress and communicate updates to stakeholders.
  • Confirm completion of engineering tasks.
  • Coordinate with Procurement regarding billing and asset status.
  • Arrange e-waste pickup for the retired PC.
  • Obtain customer confirmation.
  • Close the SR ticket.
Billing & Procurement
  • Review and acknowledge the customer's replacement request in the SR ticket.
  • Schedule the PC replacement with the customer and IT team.
  • Stop lease billing for PC2 upon retirement.
  • Start lease billing for PC1 upon deployment.
  • Update asset records to reflect the new ownership of PC1 and PC3.
  • Process all procurement-related tasks as Business-as-Usual (BAU) activities.
  • Perform a final billing verification.
  • Notify the customer that all asset and billing activities have been completed.
Engineering Tasks
  • Accept the assigned Service Request.
  • Prepare and image PC1.
  • Perform the initial configuration and corporate software installation.
  • Record all work performed in the SR ticket.
  • Back up data from PC2 with User B.
  • Restore User B's data onto PC1.
  • Configure User B's user profile and deploy PC1.
  • Back up department data from PC3.
  • Reimage PC3.
  • Configure User A's account and required applications.
  • Restore necessary data.
  • Deploy PC3 to User A on the start date.
  • Securely wipe PC2.
  • Remove the storage device if required by company policy.
  • Process PC2 through the decommissioning and e-waste procedure.
  • Update the SR ticket with all completed work.
  • Return the completed ticket to the Service Desk for final closure.
